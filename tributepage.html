Subhas Chandra Bose

Subhas Chandra Bose was an Indian nationalist leader and a prominent figure in the Indian independence movement against British rule. He was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, India. Bose is known for his uncompromising patriotism, relentless efforts to achieve India's freedom, and his role in forming the Azad Hind Fauj (Indian National Army).


Early Life

Subhas Chandra Bose was the ninth child of Janakinath Bose and Prabhavati Devi. He studied at Cambridge University and later appeared in the Indian Civil Services (ICS) examination but resigned to join the freedom movement.


Indian National Army

Bose believed that the path to India's freedom lay in armed struggle against the British. He formed the Azad Hind Fauj (Indian National Army) in 1942 with the help of Japanese forces. The INA fought alongside the Japanese in Burma and India during World War II.


Legacy

Subhas Chandra Bose's legacy is one of courage, determination, and sacrifice. He remains an inspiration for generations of Indians and a symbol of the country's struggle for independence. Bose's contributions to the freedom movement continue to be celebrated and remembered to this day.
